**The Miracles of Purim**  
*A Shiur by Rav Shlomo Brevda zt”l*  

Mesillas Yesharim, in the chapter on *Zehirus* (watchfulness), tells us that one of the greatest *tachbulos* (strategies) of the *Yetzer Hara* is to discourage us from thinking deeply about what we are doing in this world. We should simply carry on automatically from day to day, never pausing to ask ourselves, “What am I doing in this world?” Or, when about to perform an action, never stopping to think: “Should I really do this? And if so, how should I do it properly?” A completely mindless life.

What we often fail to catch from that passage in Mesillas Yesharim is that the same tactic applies even when we are learning Torah — even when we are learning the *inyanim* of Purim and *Megillas Esther shanah rabba hakodesh*. Even then, we do not think deeply.

Let me ask you all a simple question: What is the *Megillah* actually saying? It is a *nes*, an obvious miracle — but what exactly *was* the miracle?

I once spoke to a non-Jew who had just converted to Judaism. He had never seen a Jewish *Megillah* before. (Had he seen one earlier, he probably would not have converted!) I told him that in less than two weeks we have a very joyous festival. He asked if he could read about it. I said yes, and gave him an ArtScroll *Megillah*. He came back to me and said, “Interesting story, but Sherlock Holmes was more impressive.”

He continued: “You have a king with a very beautiful wife. They have a falling-out and he executes her. Then he holds a beauty contest and chooses another beautiful woman — without knowing she is Jewish. Isn’t that odd? A king should marry someone without knowing her background? Here in America, not far from here, you have the Washington Post that investigates everything on earth or under the earth — and if it isn’t there, they’ll plant it there. Yet this king marries a woman with no idea of her place of birth, her parents… nothing. He likes her anyway. What will happen in three days? The Washington Post, the New York Times, and all the rest will be full of it. ‘We interviewed her kindergarten teacher…’ (just like Hillary did with Obama’s kindergarten teacher). ‘This girl at the age of three was already selling crack…’ and so on. So the king marries her. He is infatuated with her. So what? Where is the miracle? You don’t have to go crazy.

“Now you have this poor Mordechai — a stubborn man, you know. He gets in Haman’s way, probably sticks his tongue out at him. Haman is an anti-Semite; he knows what anti-Semitism is. In college he was the editor of an anti-Semitic weekly, so he knows. He gets angry at Mordechai, and therefore at all the Jews. The queen goes in to the king, charms him, gets him angry, and has Haman hanged. You can’t marry Haman in those days, so he sticks with his queen. What is the big miracle here? People do such things every day.”

You are laughing? Let me ask you: Who said there was a miracle? What *is* the miracle? And if there were miracles, why is the holiday called *Purim*? Why not *Nissim* or *Yeshuah*? Haman cast a *pur* (lot) and it fell on the 13th of Adar — but the queen got him killed before his lucky day. Why is it called Purim?

These are not ingenious questions. These are fifth-grade questions. My *talmidim* would have to know the answers naturally. But many of you — I don’t know about all of you, some of you are stumped. Why? Because you have never really *thought* about Purim. You saw it, you heard it, and you do what everyone else does. Copycats. Not human beings.

In America, my *rebbe*, Rav Moshe Shatzkes zt”l, once said: “What is America? Sheep. Sheep aren’t human beings.” Fifty-eight years ago, when I was a *bachur* before *bar mitzvah*, he witnessed a shepherd trying to get his sheep to leave the field and enter the barn. He took his *luleh* (whistle) and blew — all the sheep lifted their heads and put them down again. He grabbed one sheep and started dragging it. The sheep next to it saw the first one moving and followed. Then the third followed the second, and within five minutes five hundred sheep were walking — following the *fissalach* (little feet) of the one in front. No one knew why they were moving; they just followed the feet ahead of them.

This is America, said Rav Shatzkes. This is what the *Yetzer Hara* wants from us.

On Purim, you don’t need a brain — you unscrew it for twenty-six hours. But I can ask you another five *kashyos* on Purim. Very simple things. You never thought of them. Don’t say “I thought of it” and put a period there. Never thought — period. This is what Mesillas Yesharim means: *Tikbad va’avod al ha’anashim bal yishuvu divrei sicham*. The *Yetzer* keeps you busy, busy, busy — and even when you have a minute, the cell phone drives you dizzy. You get used to not thinking.

I was an American *bachur* in a yeshiva high school. When the time came to go to college, my *rebbe* talked me out of it. “A new *yeshiva* just arrived from Shanghai. They learn only Torah all day. Give a year of your life to it — you’ll taste what it’s like.” He talked me into it. Then a cousin from Mir came and took me to Rosh Hashanah in the Mir Yeshiva. I had never seen *davening* like that. The last two years of *Rosh Hashanah b’Yeshivas Mir* — unbelievable.

One thing that struck me: I knew Yiddish from my European parents, so I could converse with the *bachurim*. Anything you mentioned to them, they would analyze from all angles — improve it, criticize it. I wasn’t used to this in America. Here, you read a headline and move on; don’t think, don’t think, don’t think. There, everything was thought through. They opened my eyes to the American way of life. These were people who *think*.

I recently wrote in English for the first time (my English is only yeshiva high-school level, since I never made it to college). The title: “The Miracles of Purim.” Miracles? What miracles? It was chance happenings. We don’t think. And even if I spell out the miracles, you still won’t think. Who performed the miracles? Hashem. Why did He perform them? That is the real question.

Haman was a *goy*. Who is in charge of this world — Hashem or the *goyim*? Can *goyim* issue a *gezeirah* against the Jewish people at will? Or does the *Ribbono shel Olam* control them too? In other words, Hashem sanctioned the *gezeirah*. Why? What did we do wrong?

Most of us live our whole lives with what the kindergarten teacher taught us about Chanukah, Purim, Pesach. We know very little about *Shivah Asar b’Tammuz* or *Tishah b’Av* because they fall in the summer when there is no school. Nebuchadnezzar was inconsiderate — he made the *churban* when Americans are on vacation.

Our perception of Purim begins with Achashveirosh’s *gezeirah* against the Jews. Why? Because he was an anti-Semite. Did we do any *aveirah* that caused Hashem to sanction this? The kindergarten teacher doesn’t know that. We have generations of *am ha’aratzus*.

The *Gemara* in *Megillah* says the Jews asked: *Innanu misudasai shel Achashveirosh* — “We sinned at the feast of Achashveirosh.” Rav Shimon bar Yochai answered: the *gezeirah* should have been only on *Anshei Shushan*. The Jews of the rest of the empire never even heard of what happened there. So why were they included? Because they too had sinned in a similar way in the time of Nebuchadnezzar — bowing to the idol out of fear of the king, not out of belief. It was *yiras ha’adam* over *yiras Shamayim*. The *gezeirah* was only *le’afukei* — to scare them into *teshuvah*. Hashem never intended it to actually happen.

Rashi on *Megillas Esther* and the Vilna Gaon in *Nishmat HaChaim* and *Kesav Yad* say the two *aveiros* caused the *gezeirah*. The whole purpose of *Megillas Esther* is to teach us what those two *aveiros* were and how we did *teshuvah*.

What is Purim today? People unscrew their brains a few days before Purim because they are rusty. Then on Purim itself they walk around like headless horsemen — brainless. In many *yeshivos* the *shvach* (weak) ones become the heroes because “if you don’t have a brain, it’s better.” They suddenly get a “Purim brain” for one day a year.

Did Hashem save Klal Yisrael with a whole string of miracles, turning over the world, so that we should be outrageously silly in the streets of Brooklyn, stopping traffic on Ocean Parkway and acting like blooming idiots — causing every non-Jew to curse every Jew and his father and grandfather? Is that *kiddush Hashem*? Look in *Maseches Megillah* where it talks about *simchah* — not *hilula*.

The *Avodah Zarah* of Purim is mindless *simchah*. I enjoy seeing everyone happy, the colors, the costumes, the *tzedakah*. What bothers me is us — intelligent people — seeing this and never asking: “Is this what Hashem intended? Is this what Mordechai and Esther intended?”

The *tachbulah* of the *Yetzer Hara* is that a person should never think about anything. In the back of every one of your minds there is a little *machshavah*: “Is this the real thing we should be doing?” But you don’t have the guts to say it out loud.

I have given this *shiur* in different countries — England, Eretz Yisrael. I changed whole communities. A week after Purim they told me: “You turned over the whole Purim for us. It became a *ruchniyus-dikke Yom Tov*. The *simchah* was *simchah shel mitzvah* for us, our wives, and our children. You gave us the Vilna Gaon’s *peirush*. You opened our eyes.”

From that year onward, the *bachurim* began learning *Megillas Esther*, the *maharatzos* on Purim, the *inyanim* of Purim. They are *machadshim chiddushim*. People are *thinking*.

One young man came to me after hearing the *shiur* and asked about the *Gemara*: “*Kimu ve’kiblu* — the Jews accepted the Torah again after Purim, with love, without coercion.” At Sinai they were coerced with the mountain over their heads. At Purim they were threatened with death — and they did *teshuvah*. After the *yeshuah*, they made a new *kabbalah* of *Torah sheb’al peh* and built *yeshivos*. Thousands of *talmidim* of the *tannaim* and *amoraim* came out of the “nest of Purim.”

Haman approached Achashveirosh and said: “The only way we can succeed in destroying the Jews is if their God is angry at them and issues a *gezeirah* in Heaven. Then we can issue one on earth. So let’s make a royal feast in Shushan. Provide the most kosher food (every *hechsher*!), with *shomrei Shabbos* waiters. The Jews will have no excuse not to come. In the middle, bring immoral women to circulate among the men. The Jews will have *hirhurim*, anger Hashem, and a *gezeirah* will come from Heaven.”

Mordechai warned them not to go. Eighteen thousand five hundred Jews went anyway — and sinned. Immediately the *Satan* accused: “Their heart is not with You.” Hashem wrote the *gezeirah* of annihilation.

But long before, Hashem had prepared the *refuah* — Mordechai and Esther to lead the *teshuvah*. The *teshuvah* was not bribery or running away — it was pure *tefillah* to Hashem, because they had feared man more than God. Esther told them to fast three consecutive days and nights (a *tikkun* for the *tumah* they had taken in). Two *aveiros* — two *tikunim*. Then *mishteh ve’simchah* to negate the *cheit* of lacking *emunah*.

The depths of this *Megillah* — the whole theme — people have no clue about. There were two *aveiros*. No one was contemplating *teshuvah*. This could have led to the disintegration of the entire world. So Hashem, out of *ahavas Yisrael*, began preparing the way for *teshuvah* — first with the nuclear weapon of Haman, but more importantly with Mordechai and Esther on the spot to guide the *teshuvah*.

Once we did *teshuvah*, Hashem performed even greater *nissim* — *nissim* greater than before the *churban*. And the *yeshuah* came.

That is the real miracle of Purim. That is why we have the *Yom Tov*. Not for mindless silliness — but for *simchah shel mitzvah*, recognition that Hashem saved us after we returned to Him with a new *kabbalas haTorah*.

May we all merit to think deeply, to do real *teshuvah*, and to celebrate Purim as Mordechai and Esther intended — with true *simchah* and *yiras Shamayim*.
