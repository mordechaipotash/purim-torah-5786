  
**THE MIRACLES OF PURIM**

•

*A Shiur on Megillas Esther, the Nature of the Nes,*

*and the True Meaning of the Yom Tov*

Based on the Mesillas Yesharim, the Vilna Gaon,

and the Midrashim on Megillas Esther

*by*

**Harav Shimshon Dovid Brevda**

*Rendered into Written Form from an Oral Discourse*

# **I. The Strategy of the Yetzer HaRa: A Mindless Life**

The *Mesillas Yesharim*, in the chapter on *Zehirus* (vigilance), teaches us that one of the greatest *tachbulos* — stratagems — of the *yetzer hara* is to discourage a person from thinking deeply about what he is doing in this world. The adversary’s design is that we should carry on automatically from day to day, never pausing to ask ourselves: What am I doing in this world? When we undertake an action, we should not truly deliberate whether we ought to do it and, if so, how to do it properly. In short, the *yetzer hara* desires that we live a mindless life.

What we fail to grasp from the *Mesillas Yesharim’s* teaching is that this indictment does not apply only to the mundane affairs of daily living. Even when we are engaged in *divrei Torah* — even when we are learning an *inyan* of Purim and studying *Megillas Esther* — we also do not think. The *Shana Rabbah HaKadosh* should be studied with the same depth and rigor as any *sugya* in *Shas*, and yet we gloss over it without genuine reflection.

**\* \* \***

# **II. What Is the Nes of Purim?**

Let me pose a question to all of you. What is Purim about? Everyone will answer: it is a *nes* — Purim is a festival celebrating a miracle. Very well. But what, precisely, was the miracle?

Achashveirosh was a king with a very beautiful wife. They had a falling-out, and he had her executed. Subsequently, he held a beauty contest and chose another beautiful woman — but he did not know that she was Jewish. Now, already this is remarkable. Is it conceivable that a king should marry a woman without knowing her background? Today, you have investigative journalists — the *Washington Post*, the *New York Times* — who will dig up anything on earth or under the earth, and if there is nothing to find, they will plant something. For a king to marry a woman with absolutely no knowledge of her family, her place of birth, her parentage — this alone defies all credibility. And yet, this is what happened.

Then, the narrative continues. This woman happened to be Jewish. She had a close relative named Mordechai, a *Talmid Chacham*, a stubborn man. He antagonised Haman, who was a virulent antisemite — a man who knew precisely what antisemitism was, who had been steeped in hatred his whole life. Haman became enraged and issued a decree against all the Jews. And what happened? The Queen went in to the King, charmed him, got him angry at Haman, and had the villain executed. The King could not revoke his decree, but he stood by his Queen.

Now I ask you: where is the miracle here? People do such things. Political intrigues of this nature occur in courts throughout history. A young convert, someone unfamiliar with the depth of our tradition, would read this story and find it mildly interesting — but less gripping than a good detective novel. He would not see a miracle. He would see a sequence of fortunate events, a “chance happening.”

**\* \* \***

# **III. Why Is It Called “Purim”?**

And I will press further. Even if you claim that there were miracles, I have another question. If miracles occurred, why is the festival not called *Nissim* or *Yeshuos*? Why is it called *Purim* — “lots”? Haman cast a lottery and it fell on the thirteenth of Adar. But the Queen intervened and had him killed before his “lucky day.” So what is the significance of the *pur*, the lot? Why is the entire festival named after it?

These are not ingenious questions. They are not profound *chakirah*. They are exceedingly simple questions — the sort of questions I would pose to a fifth-grade class on an examination before Purim. My *talmidim* would be expected to know the answers naturally. And yet, many of you — some of you learned people — are stumped by them.

Do you know why? Because you have never truly thought about Purim. You experienced it. You observed it. You saw what everyone else was doing and you did the same. But you never sat down and *thought* about it.

**\* \* \***

# **IV. The Epidemic of Not Thinking**

Copycats are cats and not human beings. The *Mashgiach* of Mir, Rav Yerucham Levovitz *zt”l*, once said: “What is America? Sheep.” Sheep are not human beings. He said this fifty-eight years ago. He once witnessed a shepherd trying to bring his flock in from the field at evening. The shepherd cracked his whip — all the sheep lifted their heads momentarily and went back to eating. He cracked it again — the same result. He could not make them move.

What did he do? He grabbed hold of one sheep and began dragging it. The sheep standing next to it, seeing its companion’s feet begin to move, followed. And the third sheep saw the second one moving, and the fourth saw the third. Within five minutes, five hundred sheep were all walking, following the feet of the one in front of them. Not one of them knew why it was moving. They simply followed the feet ahead of them.

“This is America,” the *Mashgiach* said. And what was true of America then is true of much of the world now.

This is precisely what the *Mesillas Yesharim* is saying. “*Tikhbad ha’avodah al ha’anashim… v’al yish’u b’divrei shaker*” — the *yetzer hara* keeps you constantly busy. You are occupied, harried, distracted. Then you have a mobile phone that drives you not merely busy but dizzy — it vibrates, it pings, it demands. When do you have a single minute to think? You become accustomed to not thinking. And this non-thinking extends even into your *avodas Hashem*, even into your study of the *mo’adim*, even into Purim.

**\* \* \***

# **V. What I Discovered in Mir**

I was an American *bachur* who had attended a yeshiva high school. When the time came to go to college, my *Rebbe* dissuaded me. He said: “The Mir Yeshiva has just arrived from Shanghai. They learn only Torah the entire day. Give one year of your life to such a thing. You will taste what it is like.” He talked me into it. Then a relative took me to *Rosh Hashanah davening* at the Mir, and I witnessed a *tefillah* that was out of this world. I have not seen anything comparable in the fifty-five years since. I joined the yeshiva.

One of the things that struck me was this: I knew how to speak Yiddish because my parents were European, so I could converse freely with the older *talmidei chachamim*. And at meals, during conversations, I noticed something extraordinary. Anything you mentioned to them — any topic, any observation — they would immediately begin to examine it from every angle, probing it, refining it, questioning it. I was not accustomed to this in America. In America, information was like a newspaper headline: you glance at it and move on. Don’t think, don’t think, don’t think.

But there, in the Mir, everything was subjected to thought. *Gedolei Torah, Roshei Yeshiva, talmidim* — the *talmidei chachamim* who had come through Shanghai — they thought about everything. They critiqued the American way of life to me. They opened my eyes. I had been seeing these things all my life, but they articulated them, assessed the causes and consequences, and they purchased me with this approach. I said to myself: these are people who *think*.

# **VI. The Real Question: Who Performed the Miracles — and Why?**

Even if I spell out the miracles of Purim to you, most people still will not think properly. You know what you should be thinking? Not merely *what* happened, but *Who* performed these miracles. *HaKadosh Baruch Hu*. And then: *why* did He perform them?

That leads to a deeper question still. Haman was able to issue a *gezeirah* against the Jewish people. But who is in charge of this world — the forces of evil, or the *Ribbono Shel Olam*? Can the nations issue a decree against *Klal Yisrael* at will, or does the *Eibershter* control them as well? Clearly, *HaKadosh Baruch Hu* is sovereign over all. Which means: the *Eibershter* sanctioned this decree. He was angry with us. He issued the *gezeirah*. The question we must ask, then, is: *why was He angry at us? What did we do wrong?*

You can live to the age of eighty and never ask this question. And if you never ask this question, you have no real understanding of *Megillas Esther* whatsoever.

**\* \* \***

# **VII. The Kindergarten Education and Its Consequences**

Most of us, if we are *frum* from birth and not *baalei teshuvah*, live our entire lives with what the kindergarten teacher taught us about Chanukah, about Purim, about Pesach. Our perception of Chanukah begins with *Antiochus HaRasha* and his evil decrees — he was an antisemite, and the story follows from there. But did we ever ask: *why* did the *Eibershter* sanction those decrees? Was there an *aveirah* on our part that warranted it? The kindergarten teacher does not know this, because the extent of her knowledge is what *she* learned in kindergarten. And her teacher’s knowledge was what *she* learned in kindergarten. Go back generation after generation — we now have multiple generations of *am ha’aratzus* in this area. It is a *mapalah*.

When it comes to Purim, what did we learn in kindergarten? *Haman HaRasha*. So now we have two *resha’im*. That is the full extent of our understanding. But even as a child, I was asking myself: my father taught me *Hashem Hu HaElokim, Hashem Echad*. How can a *rasha* do something to *Klal Yisrael* if *Hashem* did not will it? But I was a *tam*, I was afraid to ask.

**\* \* \***

# **VIII. The Gemara in Megillah: The Talmidim of Rav Shimon bar Yochai**

*Sh’alu talmidav es Rav Shimon bar Yochai: Mipnei mah nischayvu soneihem shel Yisrael she’b’oso ha’dor kelayah?* The students of Rav Shimon bar Yochai asked their master: Why did the Jewish people of that generation deserve a decree of annihilation? And they also said: *U’mah ha’aveirah she’asinu she’k’gezeirah kazos tavo aleinu?* — What *aveirah* did we commit that such a *gezeirah* should come upon us?

Rav Shimon answered them: you tell me — what do *you* think? And they responded: “If we knew, we would not have come to bother you. We want to hear what *you* say.” But how did Rav Shimon know that they had any answer at all? Because his *talmidim* did not grow up in America with kindergarten teachers. He knew that they had not gone through a single Purim without formulating a theory of what *aveirah* had precipitated the *gezeirah*. He was saying to them: You are asking me *now*, but what have you been contemplating all this time?

They answered: *She’nehenu mi’seudaso shel oso rasha* — because they derived pleasure from the feast of that wicked man, Achashveirosh.

Rav Shimon responded: If so, the *gezeirah* should have applied only to the Jews of Shushan, for only they attended the feast. Why was it decreed upon all of *Klal Yisrael*?

They said: *Rebbe*, you tell us the answer.

He said: *She’hishtachavu la’tzelem* — because they bowed down to the idol of Nevuchadnetzar.

But they pressed him: If they committed such an *aveirah* that the *Eibershter* sanctioned their destruction, why were they ultimately saved?

Rav Shimon answered: *She’lo asu ela lifnim* — they did not truly believe in the idol. They bowed down only out of fear of Nevuchadnetzar, who had commanded them to do so under pain of death. It was only an external act. And correspondingly, the *gezeirah* from the *Eibershter* was also *lifnim* — an outward threat, designed to terrify them into doing *teshuvah*. He never intended that the *gezeirah* should actually be carried out.

**\* \* \***

# **IX. The Two Aveiros**

From this *Gemara* alone, one might think that Rav Shimon bar Yochai dismissed the first answer — *she’nehenu mi’seudaso shel Achashveirosh* — in favour of the second. But *Rashi* on *Megillas Esther* states that *both aveiros* caused the *gezeirah*. The *Midrash* on the *Megillah* discusses both at length. The Vilna Gaon in *Kesavim* and the *Sefer HaNistorin* record both *aveiros*. We are compelled to understand that both were causes. And the entire understanding of *Megillas Esther* hinges on knowing what these two *aveiros* were and what their corresponding *tikkunim* required.

Let us examine them in turn.

## **The First Aveirah: Bowing to the Tzelem of Nevuchadnetzar**

In the time of Nevuchadnetzar, he constructed an idol — a *tzelem* — one hundred and twenty feet high, exceedingly narrow, which could not have stood on its own without being embedded deep in the ground. This idol stood upright miraculously, and from its summit it proclaimed: “*Ani Hu, v’afsi od — Hashem Elokeichem hevel u’rikam*.” Nevuchadnetzar ordered every nation to send delegations to bow before it. At a given signal, all were to prostrate themselves completely.

Chananyah, Mishael, and Azaryah refused. But Rav Shimon bar Yochai says in the *Gemara*: there were Jews who did bow down. Why? Not because they believed in the idol. They were afraid of Nevuchadnetzar.

But consider what this means. Is fear of a human being an excuse? You placed *yiras basar v’dam* above *yiras Shamayim*. You feared a mortal despot more than the Creator of the universe. Did any Jew do *teshuvah* for this? They did not. “I was coerced,” they said. “The tyrant frightened me.” And they considered the matter closed.

So there was an accusation thundering in *Shamayim*. The *Satan* was prosecuting: “Ribbono Shel Olam, rid Yourself of this rebellious people\!” And the *Eibershter*, in His attribute of mercy, bore this accusation silently, year after year, waiting for them to do *teshuvah*. But one day, *HaKadosh Baruch Hu* said: “Enough.”

## **The Second Aveirah: Partaking of the Feast of Achashveirosh**

Now came the time of Achashveirosh. Haman — an Amaleki, a student of history and of religions — understood a fundamental truth. He approached Achashveirosh and said: “If we will ever succeed in destroying the Jews, it can only happen if the *Ribbono Shel Olam* is angry at them. When there is a *gezeirah* in *Shamayim* against them, only then can we be their *ba’al davar*.”

Now, Haman was an Amaleki — a *kofer* and a *shakran*. When it suited him, he was an atheist; when it suited him, he was a believer. This is the way of the *resha’im*. We saw the same with Hitler: when he needed the support of religious Germans, every speech invoked the Almighty who had sent him to bring a new order to the world. Once entrenched in power, he proclaimed that there is no G-d.

But in this instance, Haman’s theology served his evil purpose. He told Achashveirosh: “The G-d of the Jews despises immorality. The Vilna Gaon teaches that there was a *mesorah* from the *Geonim* that this is how *avodah zarah* operated in the ancient world — through encouraging *arayos*. This was its mechanism.

So Haman proposed: “Arrange a grand feast. Invite all of Shushan, including the Jews. Provide the most strictly kosher food for the Jewish guests.” The *Midrash* tells us, through the *Malach* who spoke to Eliyahu, that the food was one hundred percent kosher, and the waiters who served wine to the Jews were *shomrei Shabbos*. That was Haman’s cunning — to remove every excuse for not attending. “And in the midst of the feast,” Haman continued, “you will introduce immoral women to circulate among the men. The Jews will have *hirhurim*, and the moment they do, there will be a *kitrug* in *Shamayim*.”

Achashveirosh complied. Notices were posted throughout Shushan. And Mordechai, with the *einayim* of a true *gadol b’Torah*, with the penetrating vision of one who sees beneath the surface — Mordechai saw the trap and issued a *p’sak*: Do not go. But eighteen thousand Jews went to that feast, together with hundreds of thousands of *goyim*. *Achlu, v’shasu, v’nishtaku* — they ate, they drank, they were corrupted. *Chazal* do not tell us the precise nature of their corruption. But the consequence was immediate.

*Miyad omad Satan l’katreig al Yisrael lifnei HaKadosh Baruch Hu*: “*Ribbono Shel Olam*, You are clinging to a nation whose hearts are not with You. Their *emunah* is not with You.”

Now consider: their hearts were compromised through immorality, their *emunah* was compromised through bowing to the *tzelem*. Two *aveiros*, two accusations. And the *Eibershter* issued a statement of displeasure, which thundered in the heavens. And the *Eibershter* wrote the *gezeirah*. This was before Haman’s own decree — the Heavenly decree preceded the earthly one. Haman was merely the instrument.

**\* \* \***

# **X. The Teshuvah and the Tikkun**

And yet, all was not lost. The *Midrash* tells us that the prophet Eliyahu awakened Moshe Rabbeinu in *Shamayim*, and Moshe Rabbeinu discovered that there was still a possibility of overturning the decree. He asked Eliyahu: “Is there an *adam kasher* — a righteous man — in that generation?” Now, *adam kasher* here means not merely a righteous person, but one who has the capacity to take all of *Klal Yisrael* and guide them to the correct *teshuvah*, and who will not waver for a moment, and before whom no obstacle will stand.

Eliyahu answered: “Yes, there is. His name is Mordechai.”

The Vilna Gaon explains that the *teshuvah* required was precise, corresponding to the two *aveiros*. The Jews had placed *yiras basar v’dam* above *yiras Shamayim*. The *tikkun* therefore demanded that they turn to no human initiative whatsoever — no bribery, no political manoeuvring, no flight. Only *tefillah* to *Hashem*. Because the root of the sin was that they feared man more than they feared the *Eibershter*, the *teshuvah* had to be: we rely on no one but Him.

And Esther — she was chosen to lead the other *tikkun*. *She’nehenu mi’seudaso shel oso rasha* — they had taken the *tumah* of that feast into themselves. The *tikkun* was fasting — three consecutive days and nights, which is not a normal *ta’anis*. The severity of the fast corresponded to the severity of the corruption they had absorbed.

Two *aveiros*. Two *tikkunim*. One led by Mordechai: pure reliance on *Hashem*, repudiating *yiras basar v’dam*, to rectify the bowing to the *tzelem*. One led by Esther: fasting and affliction to purge the corruption of the feast, to rectify *she’nehenu mi’seudaso shel oso rasha*. And these *tikkunim* are reflected in the *mitzvos* of Purim itself: the *Megillah*, which declares “*Hashem Hu HaElokim*,” addresses the failure of *emunah*; *mishloach manos*, *matanos la’evyonim*, *mishteh*, and *simchah* address the corruption of the feast with sanctified joy.

**\* \* \***

# **XI. The True Meaning of Purim: Not Silliness, but Teshuvah and Recognition**

If I had a heart large enough, I would pour it out to you. What is Purim today? People unscrew their brains a few days before Purim, and on Purim itself they walk about like headless horsemen, brainless people. In some yeshivos, it is the weak students who become the heroes of the day. They write *grammen*, they perform, they become geniuses for twenty-four hours — and the next day they cannot understand a simple *Tosafos* again.

Did the *Eibershter* save *Klal Yisrael* with an entire chain of miracles that overturned the world — for *this*? He gave *Ruach HaKodesh* to Mordechai and Esther and the *Anshei Knesses HaGedolah* to establish a *Yom Tov* with *divrei Torah*, *mitzvos d’Rabbanan*, *Megillah*, *seudah*, *mishloach manos*, *matanos la’evyonim*, and *Al HaNissim* in *tefillah* — for what? That people should be outrageously silly? That they should run into the streets and make a spectacle that causes every passerby to curse every Jew?

I am not angry at the people who behave this way. They are *shoggin* — no one ever taught them otherwise. I see the costumes, the colours, the happiness, and I enjoy seeing people happy. But what troubles me deeply is *us* — the thinking people. We see all of this and we do not ask the question: Is this what the *Eibershter* intended? Is this what Mordechai and Esther envisioned? Why don’t we ask the question?

The answer is the *Mesillas Yesharim* with which we began. The *yetzer hara’s* stratagem is that a person should never think about anything. And I would be able to testify that in the back of every one of your minds, there is a small thought: “Is this really what we should be doing?” Every one of you thinks it. But you do not have the courage to articulate it.

**\* \* \***

# **XII. Changing Purim: Testimony from the Kehillos**

I have had *kehillos* in different countries — in England, in *Eretz Yisrael* — and I changed them. Not with a single lecture. It took time. But they were receptive, and the *yungerleit* — the *mechab’rim* of *seforim* — do you know what they said to me a week after Purim? “You turned over our entire Purim. It was a genuinely spiritual *Yom Tov*. The *simchah* was true *simchah* — ours, our wives’, and our children’s. You gave us the Vilna Gaon’s approach. You opened our eyes. You have no idea what you have done for us.”

And from that year onward, those *yungerleit* began learning the *Megillah* properly. They studied the *Midrashim*, the *inyanei Purim*, in depth. Not every one of them is brilliantly original, but they are working on the *Megillah*. They are thinking. They are being *mechadesh*. They are producing *chiddushim*. This is a beautiful thing.

**\* \* \***

# **XIII. The Kabbalas HaTorah of Purim**

I recall a young man who came to me after a *shiur* in one of the seventeen *kollelim* where I spoke weekly in *Eretz Yisrael*. He posed a question. The *Gemara* says: *Kiymu v’kiblu ha’Yehudim — kiymu mah she’kiblu k’var*. At Purim, the Jews willingly accepted the Torah that they had previously accepted at Sinai under a degree of compulsion. The *Gemara* further teaches that in the days of Achashveirosh, a new *kabbalas haTorah* took place — *b’ratzon*, willingly. The young man asked: “But wasn’t the *teshuvah* at Purim itself coerced? After all, Haman was building gallows and holding a sword over their heads.”

I told him to sit down. I explained it as follows. At Sinai, they were compelled to accept the Torah under threat. At Purim, they were also threatened with death. But what did they do? They did *teshuvah*. And *after* the *teshuvah*, after the *Eibershter* saved them, they said: “*HaKadosh Baruch Hu* saved us when we did *teshuvah*. Let us now, as an act of recognition, accept upon ourselves a new *kabbalah* willingly — *b’ratzon gamur* — not because we are afraid, but because we are grateful.” *Midah k’neged midah*. *Meshiv tovah tachas tovah*. Nobody coerced them into *that*. The coercion produced the *teshuvah*; the *yeshuah* produced the willing acceptance.

The *Chazal* teach: *Yoseir mi’mah she’hitzlichu kol ha’Nevi’im v’ha’Nevios — she’hotzi’as ha’taba’as shel Achashveirosh*. All the prophets and prophetesses of Israel attempted to bring the people to *teshuvah* and did not succeed as powerfully as this one act: when Achashveirosh removed his signet ring and gave it to Haman. That single gesture of impending doom frightened *Klal Yisrael* into a *teshuvah* that was *ad l’Shamayim*. The threat produced the *teshuvah*; the *teshuvah* produced the salvation; and the salvation produced the willing *kabbalah* that surpassed even Sinai.

That young man’s face lit up. He said: “*Gevaldik\!* I am going now to my *seudah* with my family with this *vort*. I will say it over.” And he did. And over the years, in that *kollel* and every *kollel* where I taught it, this *yesod* transformed Purim.

**\* \* \***

# **XIV. The Master Plan of Hashgachah**

And now, return to the miracles themselves. *HaKadosh Baruch Hu*, before anyone had yet done *teshuvah*, out of an act of *z’chus Avos* alone, began preparing the means for the *yeshuah*. First, He prepared the weapon to frighten us: Haman Ha’Amaleki. But nuclear weapons alone do not bring people to *teshuvah*. Iran threatens us every other week; nobody does *teshuvah*. They shout, then they go to sleep.

What is needed, alongside the threat, is a Mordechai and an Esther — leaders who can guide the people in *how* to do *teshuvah*. The *Eibershter* therefore had a project: to ensure that there would be a righteous Jewish woman positioned next to the king, on the spot, in the right place and at the right time, so that the moment the *gezeirah* was issued, it could be addressed.

But how does a Jewish woman come to be the queen of Persia? She would have to be married to Achashveirosh. But Achashveirosh is an antisemite — he would never knowingly marry a Jewish woman. And he already has a wife, Vashti, of royal blood. He is a stable man in this respect; she is a noblewoman. You are going to remove her? It is inconceivable. And yet — it happened. Vashti was executed. A Jewish woman of extraordinary righteousness was selected as queen. The king did not discover her origins. Everything was orchestrated.

These are the *nissei Shamayim* that the *Eibershter* performed. It is breathtaking how the *Eibershter* arranged all of this so that the *teshuvah* would become possible, so that once *Klal Yisrael* did *teshuvah* there would be channels through which the *yeshuah* could come. And once *teshuvah* was done — *avadah*, the *nissim* that followed were even greater than any that preceded them. As *Chazal* say: the *yeshuah* of Purim was accompanied by miracles of extraordinary magnitude.

**\* \* \***

# **XV. A Call to Think**

This, then, is the depth of *Megillas Esther*, and the true meaning of Purim. It is not a day of mindlessness. It is a day of the most profound *emunah*, the most exalted *simchah*, the most grateful recognition of *Hashem’s* sovereignty and His love for us. It is a day when we celebrate not merely *that* we were saved, but *why* we were saved and *how* we were saved — and what we owe the *Eibershter* in return.

The *Mesillas Yesharim* began our discussion: the *yetzer hara* does not want us to think. On Purim, of all days, thinking is dismissed as unnecessary. But I say to you: you were given a brain. Use it. Think about what you are celebrating. Think about what the *Eibershter* did for us. Think about what we did wrong and how we corrected it. Think about the *kabbalas haTorah* that emerged from this near-destruction. Think about the chain of miracles that the *Eibershter* set in motion, years in advance, so that when the moment came, the *yeshuah* would be at hand.

And when you think — when you truly think — the *simchah* of Purim will be transformed. It will be a genuine *simchah shel mitzvah*. It will be your *simchah*, your family’s *simchah*, a *simchah* that comes from understanding and gratitude and *emunah*. And that is what the *Eibershter* intended.
