## Commentary of the Vilna Gaon (GRA) on Megillas Esther: Chapter 3

### Section 1: Peshat (The Plain Interpretation)

**(1) After these things** — The explanation is: In the Gemara they said (Megillah 12b) that Memuchan is Haman, and his advice was to kill Vashti so that the King would have the power to judge matters concerning himself. For if he could not judge by himself, it would have been very difficult to execute judgment upon the great princes, those higher than the "five who see the king's face." However, his advice was effective in this regard. Therefore, the King promoted him greatly because he had done two great and good things for him. 
And regarding what they said (Megillah 13b) that "The Holy One, Blessed be He, creates the cure before the blow," this refers to that which is written later, "for so the king had commanded concerning him" (Esther 3:2). The Midrash states that "The King," when mentioned without qualification, refers to the Holy One, Blessed be He. If so, what does the word "After" relate to? Therefore, they said: After the Holy One, Blessed be He, created the cure for his [Israel's] blow.

**Promoted [Haman]** — The explanation is: with wealth, as it is said, "and the man went on becoming great" (Job 1:3).

**And advanced him** — In greatness.

**The Agagite** — This is mentioned to say that the fact that [Saul] spared Agag caused this [Haman's rise].

**Above all the princes** — The explanation is: For such is the way of all kings; when the princes sit, the king does not sit among them but by himself in a place higher than them. He did the same for Haman; therefore it is written "above all the princes," meaning higher than all of them.

**(2) And all the king's servants** — These are those who attend to him.

**Who were in the king's gate** — These are the attendants who sit at the king's gate.

**The king had so commanded concerning him** — The explanation is: The King told him [Haman] that he [the King] would command thus. This indicates great affection, as our Sages of blessed memory said: "Beloved are Israel... an extra measure of affection was made known to them" (Pirkei Avot 3:14).

**But Mordechai would not bow down etc.** — The explanation is: Haman made himself into an *avodah zarah* (idol), as it says in Tractate Sanhedrin (61b): "Can one be liable for an object that is worshipped like Haman?"

**(3) Why do you transgress** — The explanation is: In a matter from which you gain nothing.

**(4) And they told Haman etc.** — The explanation is: They told Haman to take notice of this sight, that he [Mordechai] does not bow, for Haman did not see this due to his great arrogance.

**He had told them** — The explanation is: The reason they told Haman was because he [Mordechai] said he was a Jew. As it is written, anyone who denies *avodah zarah* is called a "Jew." Therefore, his [Haman's] hatred toward him was very great, for there is no hatred like the hatred of religion; therefore he spoke.

**(5) And Haman saw etc. bowing to him** — The explanation is: Haman brought himself close to Mordechai so that Mordechai would see that he [Haman] was paying attention to this. For he thought perhaps Mordechai assumed Haman wasn't noticing. When he saw that even so he did not bow, "Haman was filled with wrath."

**(6) But it was contemptible in his eyes** — The explanation is: It was a disgrace to him to kill Mordechai alone, and also because of this fact that he did not bow to him.

**The people of Mordechai** — For they told him that he does not bow because he is a Jew.

**And Haman sought etc.** — The explanation is: To uproot the religion, as will be explained later.

**All the Jews** — For it was also contemptible in his eyes to lay a hand only on those in Shushan.

**With Mordechai** — And everything was because he is "with Mordechai," meaning the Jews, for whose sake Mordechai did not bow.

**(7) He cast a Pur, that is, the lot** — The explanation is: It says "Pur" to let us know that for this reason it is called "Purim," after the *Pur*. But since we do not know what a *Pur* is, it says "that is, the *Goral* (lot)." It is called Purim after the *Pur* because this is the essence of the miracle: that he cast a *Pur*—a lot according to the *mazalot* (zodiac signs/constellations)—to see on which day he could prevail over them, and it fell on the 13th of Adar. However, the Holy One, Blessed be He, overturned the *mazalot* and frustrated the celestial arrangements. This is the meaning of "the *Pur* was overturned into Purim," and this is a very great miracle.

**Before Haman** — The explanation is: It is known that Israel has no *mazal* (astrological fate) at all in the heavens, and all the *mazalot* are antagonistic toward Israel. This means that according to the *mazalot*, Abraham our Father, peace be upon him, was not fit to have a son, as he saw in his *astrologia* (astrology) that he was not fit to have a son. But the Holy One, Blessed be He, raised him above the *mazalot* and said to him, "So shall your offspring be" (Genesis 15:5), and gave to him from the *sitra de-dchura* (the male/dominant side) (Shabbat 156a). If so, according to the *mazalot*, Israel should not exist in the world at all. From where then would Israel have a *mazal*? Rather, they are included in a "General *Mazal*." (I have explained the matter of *mazal* in my commentary on Isaiah 5:30). This is the meaning of "you have begun to fall" (Esther 6:13), and our Sages said (Megillah 16a): "This nation is compared [to the stars and the dust]." This is because they have no *mazal* at all; rather, they are above the *mazalot*. That is, if they are righteous [they are above], and if not, they descend, Heaven forbid, to the dust. But if they are righteous, they rise to the heavens above the *mazalot*. Thus, he [Haman] could not cast lots to find when there would be a bad *mazal* for Israel, for they have no *mazal* at all. Instead, he cast lots for when *his own* [Haman's] *mazal* would be strong—and this is specifically "before Haman."

**From day to day and from month to month** — The explanation is: First he cast lots on which day of the month he could prevail, and afterward on which month. For if he had cast on the month first, he would have had to cast on which day of the month for every single month. (The language here is distant and brief). "And it fell on the 13th of Adar"—meaning first it fell on the 13th of the month, and when he cast on the months, it fell on the month of Adar.

**(8) There is a certain people** — He did not mention them by name because he was afraid the King might say he does not want [to kill them] because he [the King] loves Mordechai; therefore he did not mention them.

**Scattered and dispersed among the peoples** — The explanation is: They themselves are scattered and not in one place, and they are also "dispersed among the peoples" in that they do not intermarry with the nations at all. They are separate from the nations, and there is no one who would incline to find merit for them.

**In all the provinces of your kingdom** — And they are all in your hand.

**And their laws are different from every people** — The explanation is: Therefore everyone hates them, and no one will pity them at all, nor will anyone have it in their heart to care about this.

**And they do not keep the king's laws** — A king benefits from a people in two ways: service to the king and taxes. But they "do not keep the king's laws," meaning the service of the king.

**And it is not worth it for the king to tolerate them** — For he has nothing from them at all.

**(9) If it please the king** — He said that it is not his own will, but only if it is the king's will; he is merely giving advice.

**And ten thousand [talents of silver] etc.** — On account of the tax that would have come to you from them, I am giving you ten thousand talents of silver.

**I will weigh** — The explanation is: The talent is of silver, therefore he said "I will weigh," that he would give it by weight.

**Those who perform the work** — Through those who mint coins.

**(10) The adversary of the Jews** — Since [Haman] had not mentioned which nation it was at all, [the verse] calls him "the adversary of the Jews."

**(11) And the people, to do with them as it seems good in your eyes** — The explanation is: Haman failed to mention two things: 1) He did not mention which nation. 2) He did not mention what to do with them, but simply said "to destroy them." This is why [the King] said "The people"—meaning whichever people you want; "to do with them as it seems good in your eyes"—whatever you wish.

**(12) To the king's satraps** — The explanation is: He wrote to the satraps that they should issue the decree to all the nations in the king's name, as the king writes.

### Section 2: Remez (The Allegorical/Mystical Interpretation)

**(13) To destroy, to kill, and to annihilate etc. and to plunder their possessions** — The explanation is: There are four aspects to a person: *Nefesh* (vital soul), *Ruach* (spirit), *Neshamah* (higher soul), and *Guf* (body). The *Nefesh* and the *Guf* are one, as they said in the Zohar (II:142a) that the *Nefesh* is the partner of the body. The fourth aspect is *Kinyan* (possessions). It is known that the *Neshamah* is the primary element for its *mitzvot* (commandments). 
Haman wanted to uproot everything:
1. **To destroy** — These are the *mitzvot* which correspond to the *Neshamah*.
2. **To kill** — This is the spiritual *Ruach*.
3. **To annihilate** — Even the physical *Guf* from the world, so that no remnant of Israel remains.
4. **To plunder their possessions** — This is the *Kinyan*, that they should be for plunder and not remain in one place, so that their name does not remain in the world.

Correspondingly, we were given four things on the day of Purim:
1. **Reading the Megillah** — An additional *mitzvah* was added; this corresponds to his desire to annihilate the *Neshamah*.
2. **Simcha (Joy)** — Against the *Ruach* which is in the heart, as it says "My heart shall rejoice" (Proverbs 23:15); He gave us joy.
3. **Mishteh (Feasting)** — Against the *Guf*, so that the body should benefit from it.
4. **Matanot La'Evyonim (Gifts to the poor)** — Against "plunder their possessions," so that there should now be money even for the poor.

And as for what is said, that they should be killed on one day and afterward "their plunder for booty"—for if he had said they should also plunder [simultaneously], they might not [finish] killing on that day. Therefore he said they should not take plunder until they have killed, and also that the plunder should be theirs so that they would kill with more heart because of the money.

**(14) The copy of the writing etc. revealed to all the peoples, to be ready for this day** — The explanation is: He was afraid to write publicly that they should kill the Jews. For he knew that the Jews would pursue the high offices and people would incline to find merit for them. Yet he needed to write explicitly now so that they would know, for he had to send to all the ends of the earth so they would know to act against the Jews. Therefore he did this: he wrote to the satraps and governors "to destroy and to kill," but the "copy of the writing to be given as a decree" which would be "revealed to all the peoples" did not write [the details], but only that they should "be ready for this day"—meaning they should be prepared for this day, and on that day itself, they would be told [what to do].

**(15) The couriers went out in haste by the king's command** — The explanation is: He brought them before the King, and the King commanded them to go out in haste. The verse shows us here the greatness of the Holy One, Blessed be He, who did this so that there would be time [for the salvation] twice over.

**And the decree was given in Shushan** — The explanation is: In the first place, which is the seat of the kingdom.

**And the city of Shushan was perplexed** — For they saw that he wrote they should be ready for the 13th of Adar, but they did not know what it was.

### Chapter 4 (Selected Commentary)

**(1) And Mordechai knew all that had been done** — [Remez]: He knew that they had bowed to the [image], and thus a decree of *Karet* (spiritual excision) was triggered Above.

**(7) All that had happened to him** — The explanation is: It happened because of the fact that he [Mordechai] did not bow.

**(8) And to charge her** — For he knew she was a great *Tzadeket* (righteous woman) and would not want to go of her own will to the wicked one [Ahasuerus]; therefore he had to command her.

**(14) Relief and deliverance will arise for the Jews from another place** — [Remez]: "Another place" refers to the Holy One, Blessed be He.

**(16) And so I will go to the king, which is not according to the law** — [Remez]: This refers to the "King of the World." "Not according to the law" means even without the proper spiritual preparation, because of the emergency.

### Chapter 5 (Selected Commentary)

**(1) Esther put on royalty** — Our Sages said she was clothed in *Ruach HaKodesh* (Divine Inspiration). This is because *Ruach HaKodesh* does not rest upon a grieving body [and she had been fasting], therefore she had to be "clothed" in it now.

**On his royal throne... opposite the entrance of the house** — This shows the great providence of the Holy One, Blessed be He: He placed the King in the place of judgment [the throne] but made him look toward the entrance so he would see her immediately.

**(2) And it was, when the king saw** — The Gemara says three angels were appointed to her at that moment. This is derived from the word "When he saw" (*ki-rot*) rather than "After he saw." This implies it was not natural, but by a miracle. Thus it says "in his hand" to show that [the scepter] was small, yet it reached her; the angels performed three miracles.
