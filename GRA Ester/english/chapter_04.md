## Commentary of the Vilna Gaon (GRA) on Megillas Esther: Chapter 4

## Plain Interpretation (Al Derech HaPeshat)

**(1) All that had been done** — and also that it was because of him [Mordechai] that he [Haman] did not kneel.
**And he cried out a great and bitter cry** — "Great" over this great trouble, and also "bitter" to him, for it was because of him that all this came about.

**(2) No one may enter** — The explanation is: His desire was to enter the King’s courtyard, but "no one may enter the King’s gate" [in sackcloth].

**(3) The King’s word** — This refers to the letter itself which he sent to the *achashdarpanim (satraps)*, the governors, and those ministers who had Jewish friends; therefore, they revealed it to them immediately.
**And his decree** — This is the decree that was revealed to all the nations.
**A great mourning for the Jews** — The explanation is: It was a mourning as if over a dead person, because they immediately saw the King’s decree.
**And fasting and weeping** — These are the five ways of *teshuvah (repentance)* explained in Tractate Ta’anit (15a). They said that "sackcloth and ashes" are only practiced by the leaders of the people, and this refers to the masses, and not to every single individual.
**Spread out for the many** — This implies [it was done] by others, for so they said in the Gemara: "And they place [ashes] upon the head of the *Nasi (Prince/Leader)*."

**(4) And to remove his sackcloth from him** — The explanation is: That he should remove his sackcloth and come to her so she could speak with him [to ask] "what is this," for she thought he was afraid to speak through a messenger.
**And he did not accept** — The explanation is: He did not want to separate from his sackcloth even for a single moment.

**(5) And she commanded him concerning Mordechai** — The explanation is: She commanded Hatach to go and speak with Mordechai; perhaps Hatach could elicit from his mouth "what this is."
**To know what this is and why this is** — The explanation is: One who wishes to know a matter clearly needs to know two things: 1) What is this thing? and 2) Why was this thing done? Regarding this, she asked "what this is" and "why this is."

**(6) To the city square** — To make known that he was not ashamed to cry out in the city square before the King’s gate.

**(7) All that had happened to him** — The explanation is: That it came about through the fact that he did not kneel.
**And the sum of money** — The explanation is: For he also told him to tell her this, because in the [public] decree it was not written that it referred to the Jews; therefore, he told her that the *patshegen (copy)* was against the Jews to destroy them.

**(8) And the copy of the text... and to charge her** — For he knew that she was a great *tzadeket (righteous woman)* and did not want to go to Achashverosh willingly; therefore, he "charged her." Once he charged her, she certainly would not violate the command of Mordechai.
**To implore him and to request** — The explanation is: That she should say that for the sake of her *chen (grace/favor)* he should do this, as she said: "If I have found favor in your eyes..." (Esther 7:3), and that she should request of him intensely.

**(11) All the king's servants** — The explanation is: She said that she would not be effective, but on the contrary, she would cause harm, for it is well known that "any man or woman... there is but one law for him: to be put to death." In a matter that is well known, there will be a greater stumbling block if I violate his law.
**Furthermore, "And I have not been called to come to the king..."** — And presumably, he will send for me in those [coming] days; why should I violate his law for the sake of a few days?

**(13) And Mordechai said... "Do not imagine in your soul..."** — The explanation is: Mordechai suspected that she thought one does not commit a sin so that one's fellow may gain merit [i.e., she shouldn't sacrifice her spiritual standing for the nation], and in this matter, it is a case of "be killed and do not transgress" [referring to her being with the King willingly], and she is not obligated in this. Therefore, he said that even without this, you will be killed; therefore, do not be lax, but go.

**(14) You and your father's house will perish** — The explanation is: As it says, "He who is slack in his work is a brother to a master of destruction" (Proverbs 18:9). The explanation is: If it is in one's hand to save and he does not save, not only does he not receive a reward, but he is like a murderer. Therefore, he said "you and your father's house will perish," and you—who are the Queen—all the more so.

**(16) Who are found in Shushan** — For [to gather] the whole world is impossible in such few days; therefore, she said "who are found in Shushan."
**Which is not according to the law** — The explanation is: Willingly.

**(17) And Mordechai passed** — The explanation is: That he "passed" (transgressed) the law, for he sat in a fast on Pesach and did not eat *Matzah* and *Maror*.
**According to all that Esther commanded him** — As it says in the Midrash (Esther Rabbah 8:7) that she said: "If there are no Jews, there is no Matzah and Maror." Furthermore, because she is the Queen, he is obligated to keep her command; and this is "according to all that Esther commanded him."

## Allegorical Interpretation (Al Derech HaRemez)

[The GRA connects the struggle between Mordechai and Haman to the cosmic battle between the forces of holiness and the *Sitra Achra* (the Other Side/Evil)].

There are 18 [mentions of] "Haman" in the Torah, as is known (cf. *Sifrei* 17:8). In the future, they will hang him on a tree. For in the future it will be as it says, "And it shall be a time of trouble for Jacob, etc." (Jeremiah 30:7), for then the sin of *Adam HaRishon* (the First Man) will be rectified. And he [Haman/the Evil Inclination] will be punished for the exile to this place. And to him [the Evil Inclination], the King—who is the Holy One, Blessed be He—has died [meaning His presence is hidden], and Haman has conquered everything, *chas v'shalom (Heaven forbid)*.

## Commentary on Chapter 5

**(1) And she clothed herself** — "Esther [clothed herself in] royalty." They said (Megillah 15a) that she clothed herself in *Ruach HaKodesh (Divine Inspiration)*. This is because *Ruach HaKodesh* does not rest upon a grieving body [and she had been fasting]. Therefore, she clothed herself in *Ruach HaKodesh* now.
**On his royal throne** — To show the greatness of the love of the Holy One, Blessed be He: He [Achashverosh] sat in the place of judgment and made it a "house of royalty" [mercy], and he was not in his usual designated place, and he was also "facing the entrance of the house"—all so that he would see her immediately.

**(2) And it was, when he saw** — In the Gemara (Megillah 15b) they said: "Three angels were designated for her at that time, etc." This is derived from the word *kir'ot* (when he saw) rather than *ka'asher ra'ah* (as he saw). This implies it was not according to nature, but according to a miracle. Therefore, it is written *kir'ot*, which implies "automatically." Similarly, [the word] *nas'ah* (she carried/found favor) implies that it was carried [bestowed] upon her from above. Similarly, it says **"in his hand"**—this is seemingly redundant, for certainly it is in his hand. Rather, it is to show that it [the scepter] was small, yet it was [extended] from his hand. Therefore, three angels were designated for her at that time to perform three miracles.

**(3) What is it for you... and what is your request** — The explanation is: He saw that her face had changed due to the fast, as our Sages of blessed memory said (Megillah 15b) that she was unable to stand. Also, she had come into the inner [court] not according to the law and endangered her soul. Presumably, it was a very great matter. It must be one of three things: 1) Something she lacks, 2) Something she wants to request for the ministers. Therefore, he said "What is it for you," meaning what do you lack? "And what is your request," meaning on behalf of the ministers, "and it shall be given to you"—and it will be done according to your will.

**(4) And Esther said, "If it please the King... to the banquet"** — The explanation is: She said to him, "A very great matter is required of me, and I cannot tell you immediately, for perhaps Haman will [interfere]. But if you come to the banquet, and during the banquet when your heart is merry, then I will request."

**(5) And the King said, "Hurry..."** — The explanation is: He saw the greatness of her distress and said "Hurry," so that she would make her request and no longer be in distress. This is "to do the word of Esther," meaning that her request should be fulfilled.

**(6) What is your petition... and what is your request** — The explanation is: "Petition" (*she'elah*) and "request" (*bakashah*) are two different things. A petition is for oneself, and a request is for others. Therefore, he said: "What is your petition" for yourself, "and what is your request" for others? Regarding your petition, he said "it shall be given to you," meaning what you lack; and regarding your request for others, he said "it shall be done"—when I do this with him [Haman], whatever you request.

**(8) If I have found favor... and if it please the King** — The explanation is: If I have favor, that is a sign for my petition [for myself]; and if it is good in the eyes of the King, [that is for] my request for others. "Let the King and Haman come..." for even now I cannot speak yet because of the greatness of my distress. Her intention was as stated in the Gemara (Megillah 15b). Also, she did not want to request of him [yet] because perhaps he [Achashverosh] would know the matter was [unfavorable] in the eyes of the Holy One, Blessed be He, and it would not help at all. Therefore, she pushed it off until tomorrow, for perhaps by tomorrow the matter would be known from Heaven that they agree with her. Therefore, she said "tomorrow I will do as the King says" and I will tell you.
**To the banquet that I shall make for them** — She said this so that Haman would think he was very important in her eyes, just as in the eyes of the King. Therefore, she said "I will make for *them*," but regarding the first banquet she said only "I made for *him* [the King]."

**(9) Joyful and glad of heart** — The explanation is: "Joyful" in his soul [eternal joy] and "glad of heart" that he had no fear at all. For until now, he was afraid that perhaps she was thinking against him, since she grew up in his [Mordechai's] house. But now he saw that she brought him close along with the King, and she did not bring Mordechai close; he thought she favored him more than Mordechai. Therefore, it is written now: "And Haman was filled with wrath against Mordechai." For now, what was his [Mordechai's] security? Even Esther does not favor him.
**He did not rise and did not stir before him** — The explanation is: He did not tremble before him at all. For those who are afraid of him stand before him until he passes, but Mordechai did not stir from his place as he was.

**(11) And Haman recounted to them...** — The explanation is: When a person is in great joy and great pleasures, and afterward he sees distress, even a small [distress] can cause him to die. To prevent this, he must ease the distress from his heart by seeing honor and pleasures. Therefore, Haman, since he was "joyful and glad of heart" and afterward saw that Mordechai did not kneel to him, had great distress and could have died from this distress. Therefore, he recounted to them "the glory of his wealth."
**He promoted him** — In money.
**He raised him** — In honor, as it says regarding the *patshegen* (decree) that he made for them.

**(14) Let a gallows be made, fifty cubits high...** — He said they should hang him on a high tree so that he would see him immediately. If he were just killed, he wouldn't see him afterward, but if they hang him, he will see him always. They said they should make it fifty cubits high because seeing is greater than hearing in four ways, and one of them is that the sight of a thing is greater than the hearing of a thing. Therefore, Haman, when he would be in the King's house, would not see that they hung Mordechai [if it were low]; therefore, he made it fifty cubits high so that he could see from a distance from where the King was. This advice was from the Holy One, Blessed be He, so that Achashverosh could see for himself, for "hearing is not like seeing," as it says: "Behold also the tree, etc." (Esther 7:9).
**And in the morning, speak to the King** — The explanation is: Go in the morning, and not in his usual way of going to the King as one who lacks [something]. For the second-to-the-king is usually the last to enter, but go in the morning before anyone else is with the King, so that you will be the first with the King and you will be the one to [decide] the fate of Mordechai and anticipate his [Mordechai's] request to the King. Therefore, they said "go in the morning."
**And he made the gallows** — He himself made the tree from the cedar of his house.

## Commentary on Chapter 6

**(1) On that night, the King's sleep was disturbed** — For he saw in a dream that she [Esther] endangered her soul and requested of the King, and Haman [was there]. Regarding the banquet, from the joy he had distress. Certainly, she is not endangering herself to request for herself, for what does she lack in the King's house? The joy [she seeks] must be to request for someone else. And who is this? He said: Since she has no family or nation at all, it must be for Mordechai who raised her in his house. And she also invited Haman, for perhaps he is also a friend to Mordechai and they are planning together to take revenge on me. And he said in his heart: "Who knows what she will request? For certainly it is no small thing since she endangered her soul for it." He also said in his heart: "Certainly they will say and claim that he [Mordechai] did a favor for the King, for without a favor one cannot request." Therefore, he wanted to bring "the book of records, the chronicles," to see what favor Mordechai did, so that he could respond that this is not such a great favor as to do such a very great thing for him.
**The book of records, the chronicles** — The explanation is: Not the chronicles themselves, for the chronicles are large, as they contain in writing everything done since the creation of the world. Rather, "the book of records," for they had a book in brief of every matter of the ministers, and he wanted to bring that.

**(3) And the King said...** — For he said in his heart: "Now she is requesting for him intensely, for he also has a great claim." And he asked if "honor and greatness" had been done for this, so that he could respond that honor and greatness had already been done. "Honor" in respect, and "greatness" in money.

**(4) Who is in the court?** — The explanation is: To take counsel with him what to do before she makes her request. For who knows what she will request? And also, the law is with her, as she has a great claim.
**Which he had prepared for him** — Behold, in the Gemara (Megillah 16a) they said: "He [Haman] prepared it for himself." This is not [explicitly] prepared [in the text]. It appears the explanation is: Since I have already written that the decree was from Heaven [originally] against Mordechai, and afterward it was overturned and the decree was against Haman—therefore, "he prepared it for him," meaning the decree was also from Heaven against Haman alone and not against Mordechai.

**(5) And the King said, "Let him enter"** — For he thought he [Haman] stood in the morning to request concerning Mordechai. And he said in his heart: "Who knows what Esther's will is to request on behalf of Mordechai? For Haman is also in that same [category of] punishment."

**(6) What should be done for the man...** — And he did not say "for Mordechai," for he was afraid that if he said "for Mordechai," he [Haman] would say to give him the entire kingdom, for he [the King] thought that he [Haman] was also a friend to Mordechai. Therefore, he spoke generally.
**Whom the King desires to honor** — And he did not say "greatness" as he said before [to the servants]. For this was from Heaven, that he should only say "honor." For if he had said "greatness," he [Haman] could not have applied it to himself, for what "greatness" could be given to him further? For he had already given Haman much, and he was already second to the King. But "honor" was possible, as explained later.

**(7) The man whom the King desires to honor** — But "greatness," it is impossible to give him more.

**(8) Let them bring royal apparel...** — The explanation is: For he lacked nothing but this.
**And upon whose head a royal crown has been set** — The explanation is: That apparel and that horse upon which the King rode on the day that a royal crown was set upon his head.

**(9) And let the apparel and the horse be delivered...** — The explanation is: That it should be in the days when they prepare the horse for the King to ride, and the time of the *partemim (nobles)* to prepare the horse for the transition [of the King]. And Haman said that they should do this for him too.
**And let them clothe the man** — The explanation is: His servants [should clothe him], and not he himself. And similarly "and they shall lead him on horseback" and "and they shall proclaim before him."

**(10) Hurry, take...** — The explanation is: He [the King] saw that he [Haman] said this regarding Mordechai... [text ends].
