## Commentary of the Vilna Gaon (GRA) on Megillas Esther

---

## Chapter 5

**(1) And Esther clothed herself in royalty etc.** – And [the Sages] said (Megillah 15a) that she clothed herself in *Ruach HaKodesh* (Divine Inspiration). This is because *Ruach HaKodesh* only rests upon a "drunken body" [meaning a body nullified to the physical, or in a state of spiritual ecstasy], and therefore she clothed herself in *Ruach HaKodesh* now.
**On his royal throne etc.** – To show the greatness of the Holy One, Blessed be He’s causation: for the throne is the place of judgment, and now it was in the "royal house" [the throne room] and not in his private inner chamber where he was always accustomed to be. Furthermore, it was opposite the entrance of the house, all so that he would see her immediately.

**(2) And it was, when he saw etc.** – In the Gemara (Megillah 15b) they said that three angels were provided for her at that moment. This is derived from the fact that it is written *ke-rot* (as the seeing of) and not *ka-asher ra’ah* (when he saw). This perforce implies that it was not according to nature, but rather by way of a miracle. Therefore, it wrote *ke-rot*, which implies it happened "of itself" [spontaneously]. Similarly, "she won [favor]" (*nas’ah*) implies she was "carried" by that which was placed upon her [divine grace]. Likewise, it says "in his hand" (*be-yado*); this is seemingly redundant, for certainly it was in his hand! Rather, it is to teach that [the scepter] was small and only in his hand [at first], and therefore the three angels were provided for her at that moment to perform three miracles [stretching the scepter, etc.].

**(3) What is it to you... and what is your request** – **The explanation is:** He saw that her face had changed because of the fast, as our Sages of blessed memory said (Megillah 15a) that she could not even hold up her head. Furthermore, she went to the inner court "not according to the law" (Esther 4:16) and risked her life. Presumably, it was a very great matter, and it must be one of two things: 1) Perhaps there is someone who is distressing her. 2) Perhaps she wants to request on behalf of others. Therefore, he said, **"What is it to you"** — meaning, what do you lack personally? **"And what is your request"** — meaning, on behalf of others. **"And it shall be given to you"** — and I will do according to your will.

**(4) And Esther said, if it please the king... to the banquet** – **The explanation is:** She said to him that a very great thing is required by me, and I cannot tell you immediately, for perhaps you will not grant it to me. However, if you come to the banquet, and during the banquet when your heart is merry, then I will request.

**(5) And the king said, hurry etc.** – **The explanation is:** He saw the greatness of her distress and said "Hurry etc." so that she might make her request and no longer be in distress. This is the meaning of **"to do the word of Esther,"** namely, that her request should be fulfilled.

**(6) What is your petition... and what is your request** – **The explanation is:** For "petition" (*she’elah*) and "request" (*bakashah*) are two different things. A petition is for oneself, and a request is for others. Therefore, he said: "What is your petition" for yourself, "and what is your request" for others? Regarding your petition, he said **"it shall be given to you"** — meaning that which you lack; and regarding your request for others, he said **"it shall be done"** (*te’aseh*) — that I will do this with him [the third party] whatever you request.

**(7) And she said, my petition and my request** – **The explanation is:** I will request two things: one for myself and one for others.

**(8) If I have found favor... and if it please the king** – **The explanation is:** I have "favor" (*chen*) that he should grant my petition [for myself], and "if it is good in the eyes of the king" regarding my request for others, **"let the king and Haman come etc."** For even now I cannot say more because of the greatness of my distress. Her intention was as stated in the Gemara (Megillah 15b) [to make the king jealous of Haman], and also that she did not want to request from him yet, for perhaps the matter would be evil in the eyes of the Holy One, Blessed be He, and it would not help at all. Therefore, she pushed it off until tomorrow, for perhaps she would know of something from which it would be known that Heaven also agreed, as indeed happened. Therefore, she said that tomorrow "I will do according to your word" and tell you.
**To the banquet that I shall prepare for them** – She said this so that Haman would think he was very important in her eyes, just as he was in the eyes of the king. Therefore, she said "I will prepare *for them*," whereas at first she only said "I have prepared *for him*."

**(9) Joyful and glad of heart** – **The explanation is:** "Joyful" (*samayach*) refers to [internal joy], and "glad of heart" (*tov lev*) means he had no fear at all. For until now he was afraid that perhaps she loved Mordechai, since she grew up in his house. But now that he saw she invited him with the king, and did not invite Mordechai, presumably she loves him more than Mordechai. Therefore, it is written now: **"Haman was filled with wrath against Mordechai,"** for now, upon what was his [Mordechai's] security? For even Esther does not love him.
**And he did not rise or stir before him** – **The explanation is:** He did not even tremble before him at all. For a person whom one fears, people stand before him until he passes, but Mordechai did not even stir from him, as if he [Haman] did not exist.

**(11) And Haman recounted to them etc.** – **The explanation is:** When a person is in great joy and great pleasures, and afterward sees distress—even a small amount—he can die from it. The remedy is to vent the distress from his heart and to see honor and increase in pleasures. Therefore, Haman, who was joyful and glad of heart, and afterward saw that Mordechai did not bow to him, had great distress from this and could have died from that distress. Therefore, he recounted to them the glory of his wealth.
**His greatness** – in money.
**He promoted him** – in honor, as explained above on the verse "which I shall prepare for them."

**(14) Let them make a gallows fifty cubits high etc.** – Therefore, she [Zeresh] said they should hang him on a high tree, because with other forms of death, one does not see him afterward, but if they hang him, he will see him always. And they said to make it fifty cubits high because sight is greater than hearing in four ways, and one of them is that the sight of a thing is greater than the hearing of a thing. Therefore, when Haman would be in the king's house, he would not see him when they hanged Mordechai [if it were low]; therefore, he made it fifty cubits high so that he could see it from a distance from the king's court. This was a cause brought about by the Holy One, Blessed be He, so that Ahasuerus would be able to see it himself, for "hearing is not like seeing," as it says: "Behold also the gallows etc." (Esther 7:9).
**And in the morning, speak to the king etc.** – **The explanation is:** That he should go in the morning and not according to his usual way of going to the king late, for the second-in-command to the king usually goes last. However, [they said] you should go in the morning before anyone else is with the king, for perhaps someone will be with the king who is a friend of Mordechai and will intercede for him to the king. Therefore, they said he should go in the morning.
**And he made the gallows** – He himself made the gallows, for "hatred upsets the natural order" (Bereishis Rabbah 55:8).

## Chapter 6

### Peshat (Plain Meaning)

**(1) On that night, the king’s sleep was disturbed.** For he saw in a dream [Esther] risking her life and requesting of the king and Haman, and because of the banquet she has distress. Certainly, she is not risking her life to request for herself, for what does she lack in the king's house? Rather, she must want to request for someone else. And who is this? Since she has no family or people at all, it must be for Mordechai who raised her in his house. And she also invited Haman, for perhaps he is also a friend to Mordechai and they both want to request together.
Ahasuerus thought in his heart: "Who knows what Esther wants to request for Mordechai? For surely it is no small thing since she risked her life for it." He also said in his heart: "Surely they will say and claim that he did a favor for the king, for without a favor one does not request." Therefore, he wanted to bring the "book of records, the chronicles" to see what favor Mordechai had done, so that he could argue that this favor is not so great as to do such a very great thing for him.
And it says **"the book of records, the chronicles"** — meaning, not the chronicles themselves, for the chronicles are large, as they contain in writing everything that happened since the creation of the world. Rather, it was the "book of records," for they had a book that was a summary of all the main events, and he wanted to bring that.

**(3) And the king said etc.** – For he said in his heart: "Now she is requesting for him very much, for he also has a great claim." And he asked if "honor and greatness" had been done for this, so that he could argue that honor and greatness had already been done. "Honor" (*yekar*) in respect, and "greatness" (*gedulah*) in money.

**(4) Who is in the court?** – **The explanation is:** To take counsel with him what to do before she makes her request, for who knows what she will request? And also, the law is with her, for she has a great claim.
**Which he had prepared for him.** – Behold, in the Gemara (Megillah 16a) they said: "He prepared for himself." This is difficult to understand. It appears the explanation is based on what I have already written: that the decree was from Heaven, originally against Mordechai, and afterward it was overturned and the hatred was directed at Haman. Therefore, "he prepared for himself" means the hatred was also from Heaven against Haman alone and not against Mordechai.

**(5) And the king said: Let him enter.** For he thought that he [Haman] stood there in the morning to request for Mordechai, for he thought in his heart: "Who knows what Esther's will is to request for Mordechai? For Haman was also at that banquet."

**(6) What shall be done for the man.** He did not say "for Mordechai," for he was afraid that if he said "for Mordechai," Haman would say to give him the entire kingdom, for he [the king] thought that Haman was also a friend to Mordechai. Therefore, he spoke generally.
**Whom the king desires to honor.** He did not say "greatness" (*gedulah*) as he said before, for this was from Heaven—that he should not say "greatness," for then Haman would have been able to claim it for himself. For what "greatness" could he give him further? For he had already given Haman much, and he was already second to the king. But "honor" is possible, as explained later.

**(7) The man whom the king desires to honor.** But "greatness" is impossible to give him more.

**(8) Let them bring royal apparel etc.** – **The explanation is:** For he lacked nothing but this.
**And upon whose head a royal crown is set.** – **The explanation is:** That apparel and that horse upon which the king rode on the day the royal crown was placed on his head.

### Remez (Allegorical/Mystical Meaning)

**(9) And let the apparel... be delivered etc.** – **The explanation is:** For in those days, there were those who would prepare the horse for the king to ride, and there was a time for the nobles to prepare the horse to be passed over [to the rider]. Haman said that they should do this for him as well.
**And they shall dress the man.** – **The explanation is:** His servants, and not a noble. Similarly, "and they shall lead him on horseback" and "and they shall proclaim before him." [Haman's ego led him to suggest that the highest nobles serve the recipient, but here the GRA notes Haman's assumption that he would be the recipient].

**(10) Hurry, take.** – **The explanation is:** For he saw that he [Haman] said this for Mordechai, and he thought that Haman prepared this because he was for Mordechai and said so because of his love for him. From here Ahasuerus understood that this was possible. And he said to him: "Do with him even better than what you said, and do not omit anything." And from all this, do it quickly, for immediately you will be called with Esther to the banquet, and you will be able to argue that "I have already done for him."
**Do not omit a word etc.** – **The explanation is:** All that you spoke, even what you said "my servants shall do," you do it yourself, and do not omit anything.

**(11) And he dressed Mordechai.** They said in the Gemara (Megillah 16a): "He went and found the Rabbis [learning]." But it is difficult, for how could he find them? [The GRA discusses the logistics of Haman finding the Sages and Mordechai in the study hall during the decree].

## Chapter 7

### Peshat (Plain Meaning)

**(12) And he was pushed.** – **The explanation is:** He ran quickly, for he was ashamed to stand there, and he ran.
**Mourning and with his head covered.** – For he was ashamed of the matter, like one who is a mourner for the king; such a person would be covered and in mourning.

**(13) And Haman recounted etc. all that had befallen him.** – **The explanation is:** For they thought that since he came to the king to ask to hang Mordechai, and instead the king called for Mordechai and honored him, the king must have seen that the law was with Mordechai. Therefore, he wanted Haman to lead Mordechai on the horse for this reason. And afterward, it is possible that now Mordechai will be the second to the king and not Haman. Haman recounted to them "all that had befallen him" — meaning that it was a "happenstance" (*mikreh*) and he did not know from where all this came to him.
To this they replied: **"If Mordechai be of the seed of the Jews."** **The explanation is:** Since it is a happenstance, it is from Heaven, for "Israel has no *mazal* (constellation/fate)" (Shabbat 156a). Therefore, you will not be able to prevail against him. For they [Israel] can overturn all the constellations, for they rise above to the heavens. Therefore, they are called "Hebrews" (*Ivrim*), for they transcend (*ovrim*) the constellations.

### Remez (Allegorical/Mystical Meaning)

Since "Israel has no *mazal*," they said he should prepare the gallows and he would be caught in what he prepared for his own downfall, and he [Mordechai] would make peace with him. But he [Haman] said this is a happenstance, and perhaps he could still overcome him.

**(14) While they were yet talking with him.** For it was possible that he would change the gallows he had made. Therefore, the "word of the Holy One, Blessed be He" hurried, and the king's chamberlains arrived.

**7: (1) To drink with Esther the queen.** For at the first banquet, Esther did not drink, for she was in mourning. But now she drank, as it says "with Esther the queen."

**(2) What is your petition... and what is your request.** – **The explanation is:** What is your petition for yourself, and what is your request for others, and it shall be done.

**(3) If I have found favor in your eyes, O king, and if it please the king.** – **The explanation is:** For two things are necessary: 1) That it be the will of the king, and 2) That she find favor in his eyes to grant it to her. Therefore: "If I have found favor" [personally], "and if it please the king" [the request itself].
**My life in my petition, and my people in my request.** – **The explanation is:** My petition for myself is that he give me my life. And my request for others is for my people.

**(4) For we are sold, I and my people.** She said "we are sold" because they were delivered [to Haman].

## Chapter 7 (Continued)

### Peshat (Right Column)

Ahasuerus gave Israel into Haman's hand by way of a sale, as it says "The silver is given to you" (Esther 3:11) — meaning as a gift. He did not say "it is forgiven to you," for if he gave the silver as a gift, [it implies a transaction].
And she said: **"But if we had been sold for bondmen etc."** She said two things corresponding to "If I have found favor" and "If it please the king." "If I have found favor" — you should see my affliction, for "we are sold, I and my people." And "if it please the king" — it should be according to the king's intent, **"for the adversary is not worth the king's damage."** **The explanation is:** That which causes damage to the king.

**(5) Who is he, and where is he etc.** – **The explanation is:** "Who is he" who presumed in his heart to take the queen? "And where is he" — this refers to your people. For it was difficult for him [to believe] since she said she did not know her own people. "Who is he" is like "Who is he that is pure of heart" and "Where are the hands that did this?"

**(6) And Esther said etc.** – In the Gemara (Megillah 16a) they said: She was pointing toward Ahasuerus, and an angel came and pushed her hand toward Haman. Behold, this is a great wonder: why would she point toward Ahasuerus? It appears the reason is because the Sages said that the power of imagination creates an impression on a person. Just as we see one who wants to call "Simeon" but calls "Reuben" because in his thoughts it was Reuben. Know that it is the way of all the righteous that when they speak words before a king, their heart and thoughts are cleaved to the Holy One, Blessed be He, as it says "My Master, the dream is for your foes etc." (Daniel 4:16). Similarly, in Nehemiah (2:4): "For what do you request? And I prayed etc." So too here, her blessing was to the Holy One, Blessed be He, and he was in her thoughts.

### Remez (Left Column)

[Regarding her pointing] at Ahasuerus: her thought and imagination were the reason. And she pointed her hand toward Ahasuerus [meaning the King of Kings], but an angel came and pushed her hand toward Haman.
**And Haman was terrified before the king and the queen.** For previously, he could have said that he did not know they were her family. And before the king, he could have said the truth—that they are a people, but they are evil, as he said [previously]. But before both of them, he had no excuse at all. For how could he say before the queen that they are evil? And how could he say to the king that he did not know they were her people, when he had said they were evil? This would be a total contradiction.

**(7) To the palace garden.** In the Gemara (Megillah 16a) they said: He saw ministering angels who appeared to him as men [uprooting the trees]. This is because since Haman plotted against Israel with words of falsehood and lies, so the Holy One, Blessed be He, did with him as He did to Pharaoh, as it says "And they saw the Egyptians etc." and so too with Laban. As it says "For the Lord is upright etc. and there is no injustice in Him" (Psalm 92:16). **The explanation is:** That the Holy One, Blessed be He, is upright, and what a person sees sometimes as "injustice" — such as here, or in Egypt, or with Laban — "there is no injustice in Him." It is not a great injustice, but rather they did [evil] and they are deserving of this, for the Holy One, Blessed be He, pays them measure for measure.
**And Haman stood.** – **The explanation is:** When the king went out, he stood to request for his life, as mentioned above.

**(8) And Haman was falling.** – **The explanation is:** According to its plain meaning, because of the greatness of the distress, he was unable to stand and he fell.
**Will he even force the queen with me in the house?** – **The explanation is:** According to his thought that he [Haman] wants to kill the queen and her people. Now that he is falling, [the king interprets it] as if he wants to kill her in his own house.
**The word went out of the king's mouth, and they covered Haman's face.** – **The explanation is:** He was ashamed of this, for he could not answer him. For he was requesting for his life—what could he request? For he was the one who had said [the Jews] should be destroyed, as mentioned above.

**(9) One of the chamberlains.** In the Gemara (Megillah 16a) they said: He [Harbonah] was also in that plot [originally]. **The explanation is:** For he was one of the king's chamberlains who always stand before him, and he knew Haman. But perforce, he was in that plot [and now turned against him].
